# How to Contribute to FED-EUS (Super Simple Rules!)

Thank you for wanting to help build the world's first open EUS dataset! 🌍

Your pictures will help train AI to find problems inside the body faster and help doctors everywhere — especially in South Asia.

### What we need from you
- EUS images (still pictures or short video frames) from your hospital's Endoscopic Ultrasound machine.
- Around 50–200 good quality images per center is great (but any number is welcome!).
- De-identified: No patient names, dates, IDs, hospital names, or anything that shows who the person is.
- Labels/annotations: Tell us what is in the picture (examples below).
- Different machines are okay (Olympus, Pentax, Fujifilm, etc.) — it makes the dataset stronger!

### Easy Steps to Join
1. **Get permission** from your hospital's ethics committee (IRB/IEC) or boss.  
   Tell them: "We are sharing anonymous old images for free AI research — no patient info."  
   Many hospitals give quick approval for this.

2. **Prepare your pictures**:
   - Remove all private info (use free tools like DICOM anonymizers if they are DICOM files, or just crop/erase text in JPG/PNG).
   - Save as JPG or PNG (easy format).
   - For each picture or group, make a simple note (in a text file or Excel):
     - Picture name (e.g., image001.jpg)
     - What it shows: e.g., "normal pancreas head", "pancreatic cyst", "gastric wall mass", "lymph node", "normal stomach station"
     - Size of any lump if measured (e.g., "15 mm cyst")
     - Equipment: e.g., "Olympus EU-ME2"
     - Any extra: "with pathology report: adenocarcinoma" (optional but helpful!)

3. **Send them to me**:
   - For now, email me zipped folder(s) at [your email here — add your email!]
   - Or use free big-file senders like WeTransfer or Google Drive link (share privately with me first).
   - Later we will use safer ways like Zenodo or Grand Challenge website.

4. **What happens next?**
   - I check everything is safe and fits.
   - We add your pictures to the big dataset.
   - You get credit: Your name/hospital in the README, in any paper we write, and as "contributor" forever!

### Important Safety Rules
- Only use images where patients already gave okay for research (or hospital says it's fine without new permission).
- Never share anything that can identify a person.
- We will release the full dataset under CC-BY 4.0 license (free to use if people say "thank you" by citing us).

Questions? Just message me here on GitHub (open an "Issue") or email.

Let's make AI smarter for Gastroenterologists together! 🚀
